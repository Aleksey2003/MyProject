Club history

FC Barcelona is a Catalan football club from the city of the same name, one of the best in Spain and in the world.
Founded in 1899 by a group of Swiss, British, Spanish and Catalan footballers led by Joan Gamper. The club has become one of the symbols of Catalonia. Representatives of Barcelona, together with representatives of Real Madrid and Athletic Bilbao, were at the origin of the formation of Examples.
At the same time, Barcelona is one of the three teams that have not left the top Spanish division. The other two are all the same "Real" and "Athletic".

Emblem

The emblem is shaped like a "cauldron" divided into three quarters. The top two reproduce the flag of the city of Barcelona.
In the lower part there is a leather ball from the first half of the 20th century against the background of blue-garnet stripes - the colors of the club.
In the center of the shield, on a golden stripe that separates the upper and lower parts of the coat of arms, the initials of the name of the FCB club are displayed.

Anthem

Barcelona's official anthem is Barcelona's Song, written in 1974. The authors of the text are Josep Maria Espinas and Jume Picas, the music by
Manuel Valls y Gorina. For the first time the official premiere took place on November 27, 1974 during the celebration of the 75th anniversary of the club, performed by the choir of St. Jordi.
